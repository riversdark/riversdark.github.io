> I have known that thing the Greeks knew not -- uncertainty.  (The lottery in Babylon, Jorge Luis Borges)

The name of the blog, Lottery of Babylonian variations, is a variation of the title of a short story from Borges, The lottery in Babylon. The central ideas of the story are chance and uncertainty, which seem to be quite well suited here.

The blog is mostly about Bayesian statistical modeling, especially time series modeling, so that's likely what you are looking for, and what you are going to find here. But you may also find some of my other ruminations on sundry topics.

On this blog I write in Chinese, which is my native language; and English, which I'm reasonably comfortable with; and French, which I'm reasonably uncomfortable with. If I'm writing in French, it will mostly be for the purpose of practising my language skills.
