About the blog

2021-01-01

I have known that thing the Greeks knew not – uncertainty. (The lottery in Babylon, Jorge Luis Borges)

The name of the blog, Lottery of Babylonian variations, is an (intended) variation of the title of a short story from Borges, The lottery in Babylon. As Borges and Deleuze often argued, life is all about repetition and variation. And the same is certainly true for probabilities. It’s a story about chance, uncertainty, and a mechanism that started as a fool’s play, but evolved, little by little, into a society, a religion, and ultimately the whole universe. It seemed to be quite well suited here.

This blog is mostly about Bayesian statistical modeling, especially time series modeling, so that’s likely what you are going to find here. Highlights include a relatively complete tour on Bayesian modeling workflow, and a time series modeling framework for flexible feature modeling. Right now I’m working on one post which offers a hierarchical time series modeling framework to simultaneously learn from multiple time series, and another one, a detailed illustration of the machinations of Hamiltonian Monte Carlo.

But you may also find some of my other sundry ruminations here.

On this blog I write in Chinese, which is my native language; and English, which I’m reasonably comfortable with; and French, which I’m reasonably uncomfortable with. If I’m writing in French, it will principally be for the purpose of practising my language skills. In the future I also want to pick up my Spanish and my German, which have already been left untended for years.
